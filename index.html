History

Built in 1932 by a local businessman for his wife, this 28 stall polo barn was originally built for the purpose of summering the owner’s many Thoroughbred Horses. The farm is situated on 150 acres of beautiful rolling hills, of which 45 acres were allocated to hay production. Sadly, this classic polo barn was left to dilapidate over the next 60 years until the late 1990’s when it was purchased and lovingly restored to its former glory. It now serves as a secluded family retreat and hay farm.

Products

High Acre Farm features 45 acres of top quality Alfalfa hay. Each bale weighs approximately 55 pounds. Double tied with poly twine, each bale measures 14” x 18” x 36”.

Delivery is available upon request. 50 bales per pallet, 609 bales per truck maximum (without skids) or 550 with skids. Skids can be bought for $30 each. Samples also available upon request.

Storage

Our hay is never stacked directly on the ground. It is stored on pallets on top of a gravel, not concrete, floor. This allows for good airflow and less moisture being trapped in the bales.
